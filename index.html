INTERPERSONAL RELATIONSHIPS IN THE DIGITAL AGE
A visual essay by Joe Dickson - For DES222 Task 1 - Source of images: unsplash.com
In the past, there was no technology that facilitated interpersonal relationships. To interact with someone else, you would have to be physically with them.
With the advent of mail, people were now able to communicate over long distance. It was now possible to continue a relationship with someone over a long distance, a 'long distance relationship'.
Currently, technology allows many ways to communicate over long distance. These include sending texts, phone calls, face time, and many other ways to communicate.
Today, it is possible to form new relationships through technology. This could be through shared interests, like a community forum where users talk to each other, or through shared activities, like online video games.
Future technology will allow long distance relationships to be even more intimate. A technology that is being developed currently is the friend scope, which allows people to share their vision. Source: https://www.hybrid-ecologies.org/news/friendscope%3A-exploring-in-the-moment-experience-sharing-on-camera-glasses-via-a-shared-camera
Imagination is the limit when considering how interpersonal relationships might look in the future. Sharing thoughts directly, lifelike 3d holograms, shared senses of taste, touch and smell; none of these things are impossible.
